# Daily Review — 2026-06-17

## Overall Progress

★★★★★ (9.8/10)

Today was one of the most important development sessions since the beginning of CopyStack.

The Video Engine evolved from rendering subtitles to rendering synchronized visual effects driven by AI decisions.

## Biggest Achievement

The first version of the Asset-Based Motion Pipeline is now operational.

Instead of generating primitive graphics with FFmpeg, the engine can display real visual assets synchronized with subtitle keywords.

This is the foundation for future professional editing capabilities.

## Major Improvements

* Motion Director introduced.
* Motion Asset Database created.
* Overlay Renderer implemented.
* PNG overlays synchronized with subtitle keywords.
* Motion effects now follow important words instead of random timestamps.

## Important Decisions

The team decided to abandon procedural drawbox graphics in favor of reusable visual assets.

This decision prepares the engine for:

* PNG assets
* Animated WebM overlays
* MOV overlays with alpha
* Blender-generated assets
* Future 3D motion graphics

## Biggest Challenge

Synchronizing motion graphics with subtitle timing instead of speech timing.

Several iterations were required before overlays appeared at the correct moment.

## Lessons Learned

* Subtitle timing should drive motion graphics.
* Rendering must remain modular.
* Asset libraries are significantly more scalable than procedural graphics.
