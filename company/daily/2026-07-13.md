## Problems Encountered

### Long-Running Execution

Research pipelines had become significantly more complex, making synchronous execution increasingly impractical.

Background processing became necessary to maintain responsiveness while supporting future large-scale research sessions.

---

### Runtime Ownership

Worker ownership previously existed only during execution.

Additional runtime metadata and persistent ownership tracking were introduced to improve execution stability and prepare for future recovery mechanisms.

---

### Execution Visibility

Users required better visibility into ongoing processing.

Runtime state reporting was expanded to expose meaningful execution progress without interrupting running jobs.

---

## Decisions Made

### Runtime-Driven Architecture

A strategic decision was made to separate research execution from the user interface.

The Localhost application should observe runtime activity rather than directly control processing logic.

---

### Checkpoint-Based Execution

Long-running research sessions must preserve execution progress through checkpoints.

Future recovery mechanisms should resume from the latest completed stage instead of restarting entire pipelines.

---

### Persistent Runtime State

Every runtime transition must generate persistent metadata describing execution status.

This establishes the foundation for future distributed execution and advanced recovery mechanisms.
