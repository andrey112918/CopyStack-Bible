# Daily Development Log — 2026-06-17

## Main Focus

Today's primary objective was to evolve the Video Engine from a subtitle-driven editing system into a fully modular motion graphics pipeline. The focus shifted from basic subtitle rendering toward building the infrastructure required for professional motion graphics, reusable visual assets, and future AI-controlled cinematic editing.

---

## Completed Work

### Subtitle System

Continued improving subtitle synchronization using Whisper word-level timestamps.

The subtitle pipeline now produces significantly more accurate timing by aligning subtitle appearance and disappearance with the exact spoken words instead of relying on approximate sentence boundaries.

Additional improvements were made to subtitle segmentation around important keywords, allowing the system to separate highlighted words while preserving sentence readability.

Compound Romanian words, such as **ENGINE-UL**, received dedicated normalization logic to prevent incorrect subtitle splitting and maintain grammatical accuracy.

These improvements established a much more reliable subtitle foundation for future visual effects and keyword-based animations.

---

### Video Analyst

Expanded the responsibilities of the Video Analyst AI employee.

The Video Analyst evolved into the primary analysis layer responsible for understanding the spoken content before any editing decisions are made.

Current capabilities include detecting:

* Important words
* Important phrases
* High-impact sentences
* Potential editing moments
* Editing recommendations

Instead of simply generating subtitles, the Video Analyst now determines which parts of the speech deserve visual emphasis, creating a clear separation between content analysis and rendering.

---

### Motion Overlay System

Began replacing the previous FFmpeg drawbox-based motion graphics with a fully asset-driven overlay pipeline.

Implemented the first version of:

* Motion Director
* Motion Asset Database
* Overlay Renderer

Motion graphics are now synchronized with subtitle keyword overlays rather than being positioned at arbitrary transcript timestamps.

The new processing pipeline became:

Speech Analysis

↓

Video Analyst

↓

Subtitle Director

↓

Motion Director

↓

Motion Asset Database

↓

Overlay Renderer

↓

Renderer

This architecture separates motion planning from rendering and prepares the system for significantly more advanced motion graphics in future iterations.

---

### Overlay Asset Library

Created the first version of an asset-based motion graphics library.

Initial motion assets include:

* Underline
* Highlight Box
* Impact Frame
* CTA Frame
* Top Flash

All overlay assets are dynamically loaded from the **assets/motion** directory, eliminating hardcoded graphical elements and allowing new assets to be added without modifying the renderer.

---

### Renderer Improvements

Extended the renderer to support a significantly more flexible rendering pipeline.

New capabilities include:

* FFmpeg filter_complex support
* Multiple FFmpeg input streams
* PNG overlay rendering

These improvements prepare the renderer for future support of:

* PNG overlays
* WebM animated overlays
* MOV overlays
* Blender-generated assets
* 3D cinematic elements

The renderer is now capable of handling far more complex compositions while remaining modular.

---

## Problems Encountered

The existing camera zoom implementation did not produce sufficiently smooth cinematic motion.

Although technically functional, the generated zoom effects did not meet the expected visual quality required for CopyStack.

To avoid building future features on top of a weak foundation, the zoom system was temporarily disabled.

---

## Decisions Made

Subtitle synchronization will remain based on Whisper word-level timestamps whenever possible.

Motion graphics should rely on reusable visual assets instead of FFmpeg-generated shapes.

Rendering logic should remain completely independent from creative decision-making.

Camera movement will be redesigned through a dedicated **Camera Director** rather than extending the current FFmpeg implementation.

The Motion Asset Database will become the single source of truth for all reusable motion graphics used by the Video Engine.
