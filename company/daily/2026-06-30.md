# Daily Development Log — 2026-06-30

## Main Focus

Continue improving CopyStack Video Engine cinematic intelligence by moving from random visual effects toward intentional AI-directed editing decisions.

## Completed Work

### Impact Event System V1

Implemented the first version of the Impact Event System.

The goal was to make important moments in the video coordinate camera, motion, subtitles, SFX, and effects instead of treating each system separately.

During testing, the system initially produced too many aggressive effects and SFX conflicts. We stabilized it by limiting Impact Events to camera/zoom emphasis only and removing motion/SFX from that layer.

Result:
- Impact Events are now safer.
- Voice remains clean.
- Motion is no longer duplicated from multiple sources.

### AI Director Foundation

Created the first AI Director cinematic architecture.

Added:
- IntentType
- EditingIntent
- CinematicPackage
- CinematicGoal
- CinematicRecipe
- RecipeLibrary
- RecipeSelector
- CinematicDirector
- AIDirectorPipeline
- DirectorDecisionPackage

This gives CopyStack a shared decision language for future Camera, Motion, Subtitle, SFX, Effects, Music, and B-roll systems.

### Motion Director AI Integration

Motion Director was updated to listen to the AI Director through DirectorDecisionPackage.

Added Motion Recipe logic so motion is selected from cinematic intent instead of isolated keyword rules.

Result:
- Motion feels more controlled.
- Fewer random-looking effects.
- Video output feels more organized than before.

### Motion Consolidation V1

Reduced conflicting motion sources.

Impact Events no longer generate motion.
Legacy MotionDecisionEngine is now limited to special cases like visual director or high-energy music moments.

Result:
- AI Director becomes the main source of truth for motion.
- Motion is more coherent.
- The generated video feels less chaotic.

### Creator DNA / Research Direction

Defined the next major learning direction for CopyStack.

The user collected a large reference dataset including:
- Business creators
- Commercial ads
- Motion design reels
- Kinetic typography
- Product commercials
- Editing showreels

We clarified that the future Creator DNA Engine should not only extract effects, but also understand why editors make decisions.

## Problems Encountered

- Impact Events initially created too much motion and SFX.
- Some demo visual decisions were hardcoded and made effects appear random.
- Motion came from too many systems at once.
- Several test failures happened because expected behavior changed after stabilization.
- Some indentation mistakes happened during manual patching.

## Decisions Made

- Keep development focused on one objective at a time.
- Do not add visual features only because they look good.
- Before every suggestion, ask: “Does this really help a lot, or does it just look nice?”
- AI Director should become the main source of cinematic decisions.
- Motion must be consolidated before Camera Director V2.
- Creator DNA should extract editing logic, not just visible effects.
- Localhost remains postponed until Video Engine reaches the desired cinematic quality.
