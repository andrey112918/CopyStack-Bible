## Problems Encountered

### Multiple Source Types

Supporting different research sources required introducing a common abstraction capable of representing local media, online content and future document-based research.

A unified source model was adopted to simplify future extensions.

---

### Dataset Consistency

As research projects began producing larger volumes of artifacts, maintaining deterministic dataset organization became increasingly important.

Additional metadata and standardized manifests were introduced to ensure long-term consistency.

---

### Workflow Scalability

The increasing complexity of the research pipeline required clearer separation between source management, runtime orchestration and intelligence generation.

Architectural boundaries were refined to improve maintainability.

---

## Decisions Made

### Unified Source Architecture

A strategic decision was made to represent every research input through a common source abstraction.

Future integrations should extend this model rather than introducing specialized workflows.

---

### Dataset-Centric Research

Research outputs should always be organized into reusable datasets rather than remaining isolated project artifacts.

This establishes the foundation for future AI training, benchmarking and continuous knowledge acquisition.

---

### Separation of Responsibilities

Source management, runtime orchestration and intelligence generation must remain independent architectural layers.

Maintaining clear boundaries improves scalability and simplifies future development.
